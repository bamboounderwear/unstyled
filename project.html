Museum Identity System

Identity / Exhibition / 2024

Overview

A comprehensive visual identity system for a contemporary art museum, designed to create a cohesive experience across all touchpoints while maintaining flexibility for diverse exhibitions and programs.

Client

Contemporary Art Museum

Timeline

6 months

Services

Brand Identity, Signage System, Print Materials, Digital Design

Team

Emma Müller, Thomas Weber, Sofia Keller

Challenge

The museum needed a visual system that could maintain a strong institutional identity while accommodating the diverse range of exhibitions and programs it hosts. The challenge was to create a system that was both recognizable and flexible, allowing for adaptation across various applications without losing its core identity.

Additionally, the system needed to work seamlessly across physical and digital environments, from large-scale environmental graphics to responsive digital platforms.

Initial grid explorations and typographic studies

Modular system development

Approach

We began with extensive research into the museum's history, collection, and audience. This informed our development of a modular grid system that would serve as the foundation for all design applications.

Grid System

The core of our approach was a mathematical grid system that could be applied consistently across all touchpoints. This grid provided structure while allowing for dynamic compositions that could adapt to different content and contexts.

Typography

We selected a primary typeface with a comprehensive family that could express different voices while maintaining visual cohesion. The typographic system established clear hierarchies for different types of information.

Color System

A minimal color palette of black, white, and a single accent color created a strong foundation, with a secondary palette of exhibition-specific colors that could be introduced for different shows.

The Solution

Identity System

The final identity system centered around a modular wordmark that could expand and contract based on application needs. The mark was designed on the same grid system that informed all other design elements.

Print Applications

The system extended to a comprehensive suite of print materials, from stationery to exhibition catalogs, all built on the same underlying grid.

Digital Applications

The responsive website and digital platforms maintained the same design principles, adapting the grid system for different screen sizes and interactive contexts.

Environmental Graphics

The signage and wayfinding system applied the grid at architectural scale, creating a seamless experience from printed materials to physical space.

Exhibition System

A key component of the identity system was a flexible framework for exhibition-specific designs. This allowed each show to have its own visual character while remaining connected to the overall museum identity.

Each exhibition received a custom color palette and typographic treatment within the established system, creating a unique identity that still felt part of the museum's overall brand.

Results

The new identity system has been successfully implemented across all museum touchpoints, creating a cohesive experience for visitors both in-person and online.
